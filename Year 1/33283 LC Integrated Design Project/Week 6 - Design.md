The Design 
- Identify Market
- Write a Product Design Specification (PDS)
- Create and choose concepts
- Detailed design
- Delivery

PDS:
- Basic reference to design
- Tells you what the product has to do
- Dynamic items: can be changed
- Contract: The required things by the contract which you are contracted by
- What the customer wants: Other things the customer wants
- Include NUMBERS
- Things to include:
	- Performance (what it is meant to do)
	- Environment in which the design operates (temperature, pressure etc)
	- Life (how long it should last)
	- Maintenance
	- Target product cost (to manufacture)
	- Competition
	- Shipping
	- Packaging
	- Quantity
	- Manufacture (design that makes it easy and cheap to manufacture)
	- Size (use dimensions)
	- Weights
	- Aesthetics
	- Materials
	- Standards (regulations)
	- Ergonomics
	- Customer (who the design is for)
	- Quality & Reliability
	- Shelf Life
	- Processes
	- Time-scales
	- Testing
	- Safety
	- Company Constraints (eg iconic brand features such as BMW front grill)
	- Market Constraints
	- Patents (certain designs are not legal to use)
	- Political & Social Implications
	- Legal

Six thinking hats concept:
- Change the way of thinking by looking at it only from one perspective
- Eg: only think of something as a manufacturer, then as machinist etc.
